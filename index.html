About

The Institute of Digital Risk bridges academic rigour and industry practice. We develop and disseminate frameworks that help organisations define, measure, and govern risk in digital operations, data, and technology.

Our work is for chief risk officers, board members, compliance and audit functions, and operational risk practitioners who need a shared language and evidence-based methods—not checklists that date quickly.

  • Peer-reviewed and industry-tested research
  • Structured training and certification
  • Advisory and in-house capability development

What we do

Three linked activities: research that meets publication standards, training that changes practice, and advisory that fits your context.

Research

We produce frameworks and papers on digital risk—cyber, data, third-party, AI, and platform—that are grounded in evidence and usable in practice. Outputs are published or made available to members and partners.

Training

Curriculum and certifications for risk, compliance, and audit teams. The goal is a common vocabulary and repeatable methods, so that digital risk is owned inside the organisation rather than outsourced to consultants.

Advisory

Time-bound engagements with boards, executive committees, and risk functions. We help you adopt or adapt our frameworks, run workshops, and build internal capacity so that advisory dependency decreases over time.

Community

We convene risk leaders, practitioners, and researchers to compare practice, stress-test frameworks, and keep our work aligned with how risk is actually taken and managed.

Members and partners join roundtables, working groups, and closed-door sessions. The focus is on what works, what doesn’t, and what regulators and boards are asking for next.

Case study

A financial services firm needed to present digital risk coherently at board level. Here’s what we did.

Context

The board received fragmented updates from technology, risk, and business units. There was no agreed taxonomy or single view of how digital initiatives mapped to risk appetite. The CRO needed one narrative and clear ownership.

What we did

We co-designed a lightweight digital risk taxonomy and a reporting structure that fit existing governance. Over four months we ran workshops with risk, technology, and business leads, then piloted a single board pack that replaced three separate submissions.

Result

Board reporting is now consistent and traceable to the same framework. Risk owners are named; the firm has a roadmap to fold the approach into its risk-and-control cycle. We stepped back; they run it.
